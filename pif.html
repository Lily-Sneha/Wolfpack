WHY PIF COMMITTEE ?

industry in India has remained somewhat mythical for decades. The sector does not offer many possibilities for charities to effectively pursue any collective effort around advancement of fundraising via learning, sharing, and thinking etc. Sustained and meaningful conversations between the charities, having a common goal to create a more equal world is rare. Even though, unlike the for-profit world, non-profits are way less competitive, and a feeling of kindness and respect are usually mutual. This absence of any collaborative effort to advance non-profit fundraising has led to endemic systemic weakness. National non-profit fundraising benchmark data are difficult to obtain and mostly unavailable; fundraising ethical standards are mostly left to the mercy of perspectives, ideology, or beliefs; compensation standards are inconsistent; duplicity of effort; absence of a any significant national platform for skill sharing; non emergence of any significant sector advocacy group or associations, and a general lack of progress in quality of fundraising and innovations etc. Such systemic malice has created an alarming level of trust deficit for fundraising in many organizations. Scientific enquiry in fundraising discipline is hardly practiced for financial constraints and is mostly seen as uneconomic. Fundraisers are not produced as part of any academic process or churn; hiring qualified fundraisers remains a long-standing challenge. Most charities lack the capacity or/and resources to tap the funding market. Finding reliable and relevant data on fundraising is a near impossible task.
PIF Committee exist to solve nonprofit fundraising problems and intends to emerge as a valuable anchoring institution, dedicated to improving the nonprofit fundraising eco-system. We are an agency for collecting and distributing news and information, coordinating work, or performing specified services on charitable giving and charitable fundraisers. As a nonprofit fundraising specialist for research, development and applied fundraising practices, PIF Committee works with charities and other Development Fundraising organizations (e.g. private Universities) to improve their fundraising operations and the overall fundraising ecosystem.

WHO IS PIF COMMITTEE ?

PIF Committee has been created to address critical issues or solve long-standing problems that are hindering fundraising work for public interest (any action or policies that are for public good). We possibly exist in the most diverse and dramatic fundraising dancefloor in the world. Solving some of these fundraising problems may lead to significant human progress, driven by the charities. The PIF Committee possess over one hundred years of collective nonprofit fundraising experience in Asia, Western Europe and North America. With priceless education from uncountable days and nights of small and significant failures, we take pride in our own integrity standards and wear an unwavering resilient attitude to solve systemic problems in fundraising.

HOW does PIF Committee work ?

Fundraising Ecosystem Projects (regular sectoral programs):

  • Periodic Nonprofit fundraising landscaping
  • Training and certification of fundraising professionals (in association with globally acclaimed institutions)
  • Data consolidation and analytics
  • Developing open sourcing tools and other fundraising materials

Development Collaboratives for Fundraising Advancement Projects (regular sectoral programs):

  • National nonprofit fundraising benchmarking
  • Biennial India Fundraising Festival
  • Fundraising transparency and credibility enhancement
  • Work towards an association of non-profits

Fundraising Growth Projects:

  • Fundraising recruitment
  • Classical fundraising- High net worth fundraising; Corporate fundraising; Trusts and foundation fundraising; Digital fundraising; Retail (individual/household) fundraising, Cause related marketing; Merchandise; Middle donor fundraising; Cult fundraising
  • Applied fundraising- developing and testing fundraising products; output-oriented fundraising training; Fortifying fundraising operation; capacity building of fundraising unit or individuals
  • Strategic- Fundraising strategy refresh and/or development; Fund development and financing strategy; Assessment of fundraising operations; Donor engagement strategy; triggering fundraising growth; cultural alignment to influence productivity and operational challenges; developing fundraising operation as an engine for sustainability.
